üéØ The Problem

When I joined Stripe's Revenue Operations team, the GTM organization was struggling with:

1. Approval Bottlenecks

  • No standardized review process for cross-functional initiatives
  • Decisions taking 7-10 days due to unclear routing and ownership
  • Executive stakeholders (VP GTM, Finance, Risk Leadership) involved ad-hoc

2. Lack of Transparency

  • No visibility into who was reviewing what
  • Stakeholders unaware of pending decisions affecting their work
  • Repeated meetings to get the same approvals

3. Regional Coordination Challenges

  • 8 initiatives running across Ireland, US, and Singapore
  • Time zone differences causing additional delays
  • Inconsistent documentation and decision logs
Business Impact:
  • Product launches delayed by 2-3 weeks on average
  • Revenue operations decisions stalled
  • Engineering resources blocked waiting for business approvals
  • Executive time wasted in repetitive approval meetings

üîç My Approach

I took a structured 4-phase approach to design and implement the governance framework:

Phase 1: Discovery & Stakeholder Mapping (Weeks 1-3)

  • Conducted 15+ stakeholder interviews with GTM Leadership, Finance, Risk, Compliance, Product, and Engineering
  • Mapped decision workflows for 8 concurrent initiatives
  • Identified approval dependencies and bottlenecks
  • Analyzed historical approval timelines (avg 7-10 days)
Key Insight: 60% of delays were caused by unclear routing, not actual decision-making time.

Phase 2: Framework Design (Weeks 4-6)

  • Designed tiered approval framework based on initiative impact and risk
  • Created decision matrix defining who needs to approve, when approvals are required, and what information is needed
  • Built approval routing logic in Asana
  • Established SLAs for each approval tier:
    • Tier 1 (Low risk): 24 hours
    • Tier 2 (Medium risk): 48 hours
    • Tier 3 (High risk/exec): 72 hours

Phase 3: Pilot & Iteration (Weeks 7-10)

  • Piloted framework with 3 initiatives across different regions
  • Gathered feedback from 8 executive stakeholders
  • Refined pre-read templates based on exec feedback
  • Adjusted routing logic to account for time zones
Pilot Results:
  • Approval time reduced by 3 days during pilot phase
  • 90% of stakeholders reported improved visibility
  • Zero missed approvals during pilot period

Phase 4: Rollout & Training (Weeks 11-16)

  • Launched framework across all 8 GTM initiatives
  • Trained 25+ project coordinators and PMs on new process
  • Created Confluence documentation and playbooks
  • Set up automated Slack notifications for pending approvals
  • Established bi-weekly governance reviews with VP GTM

üöß Challenges & Learnings

Challenge 1: Executive Resistance to Standardization

Problem: Some executives initially resisted fixed approval timelines, preferring ad-hoc decision-making.

Solution: Demonstrated time savings using pilot data. Emphasized that framework provides structure while allowing flexibility for urgent decisions.

Result: 100% exec adoption after pilot.

Challenge 2: Time Zone Coordination

Problem: Coordinating approvals across Ireland (GMT), US (PST), and Singapore (SGT) created 16-hour time zone spread.

Solution: Built async review capability with 24-hour SLA for non-urgent approvals. Scheduled critical reviews at 8am PST (optimal overlap).

Result: Zero delays due to time zones post-launch.

Challenge 3: Balancing Speed vs. Thoroughness

Problem: Pressure to move fast sometimes conflicted with need for comprehensive reviews.

Solution: Created tiered framework where low-risk initiatives had expedited reviews while high-risk initiatives maintained thorough process.

Result: Maintained quality while reducing approval timeline.

üí° Key Takeaways

  1. Process Design > Tool Selection: The framework succeeded because we focused on stakeholder needs first, then selected tools to support the process.
  2. Executive Buy-in Through Data: Pilot results were more persuasive than theoretical benefits. Always pilot and measure before full rollout.
  3. Flexibility Within Structure: The framework provided guardrails while allowing executives to escalate urgent decisions. This balance was critical for adoption.
  4. Regional Coordination Requires Async Capability: Time zones can't be solved by scheduling alone. Building async processes was essential for 3-region coordination.
